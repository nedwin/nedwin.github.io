# About page — opening variants

The first 1-3 sentences are the most important on the site. They set the voice for everything else. Below are 8 candidate openings across four registers — pick what feels true, remix freely, or mark ones to throw out.

Every variant is followed by what the rest of the page would lead into, so you can judge how the opening sets up the body.

A few facts I'm working from (correct any I have wrong):
- Australian, originally from Melbourne, in San Francisco since ~2014
- Co-founded Great Question (current chapter), raised Series A in late 2025
- Previously founded Elto (freelance web developer marketplace, acquired by GoDaddy 2015)
- Before that, Native Digital (digital agency, 2010-2012)
- Married to Megan; proposed at Byron Bay in 2018
- Family in Melbourne — nieces and a nephew; you fly home a few times a year
- A runner; have written about it before
- The "Seek rejection" essay (2011) is the piece people quote back to you

## Register A — Functional / direct
*The 2020 site voice, updated. "Founder, product manager, runner." energy. Three nouns and no fluff.*

### A1
> I'm Ned. I'm Australian, I live in San Francisco, and I co-founded a UX research company called [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co).
>
> Before that I built Elto, which got acquired by GoDaddy in 2015. Before that I ran a digital agency in Melbourne. Before that I sold things on eBay.

### A2
> Founder. Australian. San Franciscan since 2014. Running [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co) — a UX research platform — with a team I love. Previously: [Elto](#) (acquired by GoDaddy, 2015), Native Digital, a small pile of failed ideas, and a worse-than-average eBay business when I was twelve.

## Register B — Warm / personal
*Leads with the human, not the resume. More texture. The body has room for everything else.*

### B1
> I'm Ned Dwyer. I'm an Australian who's been in San Francisco long enough to feel like a local but not long enough to stop noticing the fog. I co-founded [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co) and I spend most of my hours there. The rest goes to my wife Megan, our cat, long runs through the Presidio, and the nieces and nephew I fly home to Melbourne to see whenever I can.

### B2
> Hello — I'm Ned.
>
> I grew up in Melbourne, moved to San Francisco in 2014 with a startup I'd just sold, and somehow never left. I'm married to Megan. I run, badly and far. I co-founded [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co), which is what I think about most days.
>
> This site is for the writing I've been doing on the side for fifteen years and never had a real home for.

## Register C — Literary / distinctive
*Jon Williams "you are not the sea" energy — one opening line that doesn't sound like every other founder bio. Higher risk, higher memorability if it lands.*

### C1
> I came to San Francisco to sell a company I no longer wanted, and stayed for the second one, which I still do.
>
> I'm Ned Dwyer. I co-founded [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co). This is where I write down what I think when I think it's worth writing down.

### C2
> Most of what I know about building things, I learned from being told no.
>
> I'm an Australian, a husband, a founder, a runner, and a believer that the long middle is where everything actually happens. I co-founded [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co). I've been writing about startups and rejection and running for about fifteen years, mostly on other people's platforms. This is mine.

### C3
> *Seek rejection.* I wrote those two words on a blog in 2011 and they've followed me around ever since.
>
> Australian. San Franciscan. Husband to Megan. Co-founder of [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co). Formerly the founder of [Elto](#), which GoDaddy acquired in 2015. Currently building the thing I'd want to use, with people I'd want to work with, in a city I'd want to live in.

## Register D — Combination (the one I think is closest to right)
*Functional opening (so people get who you are in 5 seconds), then a warmer paragraph (so they get a sense of who you are), then optional literary close. Hedges the bet.*

### D1
> I'm Ned Dwyer. I co-founded [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co), a UX research platform used by teams at Canva, Notion, Intuit and a few hundred other companies. We raised a Series A in late 2025 and we're rebuilding the whole product for the AI-native era. It's the most fun I've had at work.
>
> Before this I founded [Elto](#), a marketplace for freelance web developers, which was acquired by GoDaddy in 2015. Before *that* I ran a small digital agency in Melbourne called Native Digital. I'm Australian, I've been in San Francisco since 2014, and the move was meant to be temporary.
>
> Off-hours: married to Megan, family in Melbourne (nieces Poppy, Quinn and Mira; nephew Wolfe), long runs in the Presidio, and a steady appetite for the kind of writing that doesn't have an obvious place to publish itself — which is partly why this site exists.

### D2
> I'm Ned Dwyer — Australian, San Franciscan, co-founder of [Great Question](https://greatquestion.co), husband to Megan, uncle to four, slow runner.
>
> Most of my working life has been about building software companies. I founded Elto (acquired by GoDaddy, 2015), then Great Question (raised a Series A in late 2025). Before all of that I ran a small agency in Melbourne and did a couple of years that didn't work out, which I'll get to.
>
> I've been writing on and off since 2011 — mostly about startups and rejection and the middles of things — and this is the first time I've had it all in one place.

## What comes after the opening

Regardless of which opener wins, the rest of the page would have small lists riffing on the originals from the 2020 site (which were charming and worth keeping):

- **Currently working on** — GQ's AI-native reset; Series A growth motion; one or two side bets
- **Previously** — Great Question · Elto (acq. GoDaddy 2015) · Native Digital · GoDaddy Pro Connect · brief stint at Spritz
- **Ideas I think about** — refreshed version of the 2020 list; some of those (safety nets for the automated workforce, partner programs as a service) still apply
- **Random things I've built** — brb.life, Booooooooooom.com, Duuuuuude.com, Dampcats (RIP), etc.
- **Greatest failures** — Mr Chop (2006), Orgnition (2007), and a few new entries from the GQ era
- **How to reach me** — email, X, LinkedIn

---

## My take

I'd ship **D1** — functional first paragraph (signals competence in 5 seconds for the founder/investor/operator audience), warmer middle paragraph (Australian, SF, partner), human close (running, family, writing) that earns the reader the right to keep going. It lets the same page serve a Lenny's Newsletter reader and your nieces.

If you want a more distinctive lede, **C1** has the most memorable opening sentence — "I came to San Francisco to sell a company I no longer wanted, and stayed for the second one, which I still do" — but you'd be losing the "what does Ned actually do" signal in the first line.
