People I like and admire. Some I've known for the better part of twenty years; some do work I read the moment it lands. All of them are worth your time. I'll add to this as I go.

- **[Jesse Hanley](https://jessehanley.com)** ([@jessethanley](https://x.com/jessethanley)). I've been lucky to have watched Jesse go from S-tier marketer to S-tier developer, entrepreneur, and inventor. His feed is my favorite source for pragmatic AI development practices, and he's building a one-of-a-kind business in a crowded space at [Bento](https://bentonow.com/).
- **[Jonathan Williams](https://jonathannen.com)** ([@jonathannen](https://x.com/jonathannen)). Multi-time founder ([Culture Amp](https://www.cultureamp.com), now [Pyn](https://www.pynhq.com)) and one of the most experienced and successful founders I know. Lately he's writing sharply on [his approach to software development and the future of AI](https://jonathannen.com).
- **[Mike Overell](https://moverell.com)** ([@mboverell](https://x.com/mboverell)). One of the first people I met when I landed in SF, and a great friend & advisor ever since. I admire how he does all of it: as a family man, a friend, a founder & operator. Most recently side questing on [New Literacies](https://newliteracies.ai) to work out what kids need to thrive in a world shaped by AI.
- **[Nick Crocker](https://www.nickcrocker.com)** ([@nickcrocker](https://x.com/nickcrocker)). An inspiration since I first got to know him some 20 years ago. An operator turned investor: he built products on the [internet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/We_Are_Hunted) and is now a general partner at [Blackbird](https://www.blackbird.vc), backing the best of the best. [His newsletter](https://nickcrocker.substack.com), on the random beauty he finds online, is an instant must-read every time it lands in my inbox, about once a quarter.
- **[Nick Gray](https://nickgray.net)**. The internet's friend. I love the energy he brings to everything, from Museum Hack to *The 2-Hour Cocktail Party*, and that he's always experimenting with new ideas, including the [/friends page](https://slashfriends.org) that inspired this one.
